# sharvilpatel.dev

## Product Purpose

Personal portfolio and recruiting tool for Sharvil Patel, a software engineer. The site's job is to get a recruiter or hiring manager from landing to "this person is worth a call" in under two minutes: project writeups demonstrate engineering depth, the resume page gives the formal record, and occasional articles show how he thinks.

## Register

brand

The design IS the product here. A portfolio for an engineer is itself evidence of judgment and craft. The site must read as deliberately designed, not templated.

## Users

- Technical recruiters skimming dozens of candidate sites a day. They need name, positioning, and proof-of-work above the fold, and a resume one click away.
- Hiring managers and senior engineers doing a deeper pass. They read one or two project writeups end to end and judge the reasoning.
- Future: readers arriving at individual blog posts from links or search.

## Brand & Tone

Swiss/brutalist-lite. Strong visible grid, ruled and numbered sections, bold tight-set sans headings, monospace metadata, one signal accent color, sharp edges everywhere. High contrast, zero decoration, zero client-side JavaScript. The restraint is the statement: everything on screen is structure or content, nothing is ornament.

Copy is plain, confident, first-person, and short. No buzzwords, no "passionate about."

## Anti-references

- Default developer-portfolio templates (gradient hero, emoji wave, three identical project cards with GitHub stars).
- Glassmorphism, drop shadows, rounded corners, gradient text.
- Dark-mode-by-default "hacker" aesthetic.
- Anything that looks like a Notion page or a resume builder export.

## Strategic principles

1. Speed and accessibility are brand attributes: static HTML, system fonts, AA contrast, perfect Lighthouse scores.
2. Project writeups are the centerpiece; every page should route attention toward them or the resume.
3. Content is markdown; adding a post or project must never require touching layout code.
